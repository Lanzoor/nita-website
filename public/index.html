Welcome to the Nippon Initial Teaching Alphabet project

The Nippon Initial Teaching Alphabet project is a project aiming to teach Japanese kids English through the Initial Teaching Alphabet, a variant of the English Alphabet designed to make spelling a bit more consistent before handling the complex world of Traditional Orthography.

About the Initial Teaching Alphabet

What is ITA?

The Initial Teaching Alphabet is a writing system of English that represents the 44 sounds of spoken English.

It was designed by Sir James Pitman, to give beginning readers a logical and reliable reading and writing system.
